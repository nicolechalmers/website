Nicole Chalmers

Social Entrepeneur & Product Developer

Introduction

Nicole is a social entrepreneur who is passionate about levelling life's playing-field by making education and medicine accessible to everyone regardless of gender, race, geography, or background.

Nicole specialises in research-driven product development. She dives deep into a problem, understanding its complexities and subtleties, and designs innovative people-focussed solutions. She has led the product development of a number of projects including Meducation, Exercism, Kaido, and Reachora. Over 200,000 people have benefited from free educational products that Nicole has developed.

Nicole is the co-founder of Thalamus where she leads a variety of projects, ranging from mentor-based learning for junior software developers to artificial intelligence used during diagnostic heart surgery. Thalamus is a profitable, independently owned company.

Notable Work

Thalamus

Nicole co-founded Thalamus in 2017, with the aim of solving meaningful, real-world problems through the use of artificial intelligence. In its first year, Thalamus has created solutions for Kaido, RedBrick Bills, and Big Wide World, and been shortlisted for New Business of the Year.

Nicole currently splits her time between building Reachora - a platform for medical publishers - and developing an artificial intelligence algorithm for use during diagnostic heart-surgery, which if successful will result in improved outcomes for 150,000 patients worldwide each year.

Meducation

Nicole joined Meducation after their seed round as Head of Product. She led Meducation's development from a small UK site to an internationally respected platform used by 15% of medical students worldwide.

Meducation provides thousands of free learning resources to medical students, supplementing their formal university learning, and providing access to high-quality teaching material for students from all over the world. It is 100% free.

Exercism

Exercism was founded in 2012 by Katrina Owen as a platform to help budding software developers improve their coding skills through mentorship and practice.

Thalamus took a 30% stake in the company in 2017. Nicole led a full product redevelopment, designing the platform to be friendly, welcoming and accessible to anyone regardless of geography. Exercism is now used by 150,000 software developers worldwide. It is 100% free.
